Customer Service Experience (CSI) Dashboard

Objective:
The Customer Service Index (CSI) Dashboard currently gathers and analyzes all customer service activities—such as phone calls, support tickets, service outages, and operational work to create a single, clear view of each customer’s service experience. Based on this data, every customer is rated as Excellent, High, Medium, Poor, or Very Poor.
The goal now is to extend this CSI Dashboard to support Technical Assistance Center (TAC) Level-1 teams and make customer experience information instantly visible where agents need it most.
Primary Objective
To show a customer’s overall service experience directly on the main CRM Billing Summary page, so TAC Level-1 agents can immediately understand how the customer has been treated in the past. This quick insight helps agents respond with greater awareness, empathy, and more effective technical query resolution.
To achieve this:
A Service Experience Rating tag (e.g., Excellent, High, Medium, Poor and very poor) will be displayed on the CRM Billing Summary page.
A Customer Experience Journey Bar will visually show the customer’s service history at glance with required solution.

Secondary Objective

To provide easy-to-understand charts, graphs, and filters that give a high-level view of customer service experience across the entire company. This will help teams quickly see trends, identify problem areas, and understand overall customer satisfaction percentage/count.
Customized Tickets/calls/outages/activities scoring option should be provided like Najoomi Dashboard.

City:
The system will display a city-level pie chart.
When a city is selected, the system will display a pie chart of all areas within that city, showing the selected customer category percentage/count relative to the total active customer in each city.
When an area is selected, it will display very poor customer service information for its sub-areas.
In short: The selected customer service category will show its percentage/count relative to the total active customer in the city, its area, and sub-area levels, and provide a ranking of very poor customers based on the active customer percentages/count in every city/area/sub-area wise.
Image Reference 
For example, when Lahore is selected, the system will display a pie chart of all areas within Lahore. When an area is selected, it will display a pie chart of its sub-areas, showing the very poor customer percentage/count relative to the total active customer in that area or sub-area.
In short: The drill-down shows very poor customer percentages/count at the city → area → sub-area level based on active customer.
